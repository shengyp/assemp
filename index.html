3. Case Study

3.1 Algebra Example

Algebraic problem solving is one of the most important modules of elementary mathematics. Its main feature is that mathematical problems contain rigorous problem solving logic and complex symbolic calculations. At the same time, algebra problems are very skillful, and they are often a type of problem. Changing the value will change the way of calculation. Therefore, in order to solve the algebra problem, we must accumulate relevant problem-solving skills in addition to the common knowledge points. Only in this way can we solve algebraic problems with diversity. Therefore, we have chosen this technical and computational subject.
Here is the function $ f(x)=sin(x)-2\sqrt{3}*sin(\frac{x}{2})^{2} $.
(1) Get the minimum cycle of function $ f(x) $.
(2) Get the minimum value of function $ f(x) $ at $ [0,\frac{2π}{3}] $.

After being processed by NLP, we can get following relations.

Question Relations:

NO. Relations
1 CommonQustionRelations:
2 CommonFunctionRelation{function:$ f(x)=sin(x)-2\sqrt{3}*sin(\frac{x}{2})^{2} $,definitionDomain:R}
3 SubstemRelation:
4 (1) None
5 (2)$ x∈[0,\frac{2π}{3}] $
6 SubConclusionRelation：
7 (1) [SolutionConclusionRelation{relation:getMinClycle，CommonFunctionRelation{function:
$ f(x)=sin(x)-2\sqrt{3}*sin(\frac{x}{2})^{2} $,definitionDomain:R}}]
8 (2) [SolutionConclusionRelation{relation:getMinValue，CommonFunctionRelation{function:
$ f(x)=sin(x)-2\sqrt{3}*sin(\frac{x}{2})^{2} $,definitionDomain:R}}]

And,here is the solving result which contains a very detailed process of solving problems.

AutoSolving Result:

NO. Solving Result
1
(1)∵ $ f(x)=sin(x)-2\sqrt{3}*sin(\frac{x}{2})^{2} $
(2)∴ by (1): $ sin(x)-2\sqrt{3}*sin(\frac{x}{2})^{2} $
(3)∴ by (2): $ sin(x)-2\sqrt{3}+2\sqrt{3}*cos(\frac{x}{2}) $
(4)∴ by (3): $ sin(x)-\sqrt{3}+\sqrt{3}*cos(x) $
(5)∴ by (4): $ 2*sin(x-\frac{π}{3})-\sqrt{3} $
(6)∴ by (1) and (5): $ f(x) = 2*sin(x-\frac{π}{3})-\sqrt{3} $
(7)∴ so,we can get the min cycle from (6): $ 2π $ is the min cycle of the function $ f(x) = 2*sin(x-\frac{π}{3})-\sqrt{3} $.
2
(1)∵ $ f(x)=sin(x)-2\sqrt{3}*sin(\frac{x}{2})^{2} $
(2)∴ by (1): $ sin(x)-2\sqrt{3}*sin(\frac{x}{2})^{2} $
(3)∴ by (2): $ sin(x)-2\sqrt{3}+2\sqrt{3}*cos(\frac{x}{2}) $
(4)∴ by (3): $ sin(x)-\sqrt{3}+\sqrt{3}*cos(x) $
(5)∴ by (4): $ 2*sin(x-\frac{π}{3})-\sqrt{3} $
(6)∴ by (1) and (5): $ f(x) = 2*sin(x-\frac{π}{3})-\sqrt{3} $
(7)∵ $ x∈[0,\frac{2π}{3}] $
(8)∴ by (6) and (7): $ x+\frac{π}{3}∈[\frac{π}{3},π] $
(9)∴ by (8):$ 2sin(x-\frac{π}{3})-\sqrt{3}∈[-\sqrt{3},2-\sqrt{3}] $
(10)∴ so, we can get the min value from (9):The minimum value of $ f(x) = 2*sin(x-\frac{π}{3})-\sqrt{3} $ at $ x=\frac{2π}{3} $.
The above examples are presented in terms of natural language understanding and human-like problem solving. First, we enter the question number to get the specific topic content. Secondly, the natural language processing module analyzes and understands the topics and conclusions and translates them into corresponding knowledge relationships. This forms the three relationships of CommonQustionRelations, SubstemRelation and SubConclusionRelation in the Question Relations table, each of which contains one or more specific knowledge relationships. Then, the reasoning module receives the result of the natural language processing, and inserts the common problem, the sub-question and the sub-conclusion relationship into the knowledge base for reasoning. We can see from the above AutoSolving Result table that the solution process is based on a strict logical relationship step by step. Finally stop the reasoning and output the result. This forms a complete solution process.

3.2 Geometry Example

The main key to answering the geometric problem lies in its rigorous logic. The conclusion of the latter step is derived from the conditions of the previous step. This must be supported by sufficient evidence. The conclusions to be proved or solved from the problem to the end must have a very rigorous derivation process. This aspect requires a large number of formulas and theorems, and on the other hand we have the ability to use them flexibly. Only in this way can we solve such problems. Here is an Olympic math problem for 2D geometry.
Suppose point $ A,B,C $ and $ D $ are four different points arranged in turn on a straight line, the line intersects with the circle $ O $ having a diameter $ AC $ at point $ X $ , and intersects with the circle $ Q $ having a diameter $ BD $ at point $ Y $. Line $ XY $ intersects $ BC $ with point $ Z $,if point $ P $ is a point different from $ Z $ on line $ XY $ ,the line $ CP $ intersects with the circle $ O $ having a diameter $ AC $ at point $ C $ and $ M $ ,the line $ BP $ intersects with the circle $ Q $ having a diameter $ BD $ at point $ B $ and $ N $.Try to prove : $ AM, XY $ and $ DN $ three lines intersect at one point.

After being processed by NLP, we can get following relations.

Question Relations:

NO. Relations
1 CommonQustionRelations:
2 DiameterRelation{diameter=AC, circle=Circle[O]{center=O, analytic=y_O=f(x_O), length=null}
3 DiameterRelation{diameter=BD, circle=Circle[Q]{center=Q, analytic=y_Q=f(x_Q), length=null}
4 PointRelation:A, PointRelation:B, PointRelation:C
5 LineCrossCircleRelation{line=CP, circle= O, crossPoints=[C, M], crossPointNum=2}
6 LineCrossCircleRelation{line=BP, circle= Q, crossPoints=[B, N], crossPointNum=2}
7 SubstemRelation:
8 None
9 Conclusion:
10 ProveConclusionRelation:[MultiLineCrossRelation{lines=[DN,AM,XY]}]
